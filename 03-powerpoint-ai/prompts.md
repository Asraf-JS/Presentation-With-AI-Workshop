# 03 — PowerPoint AI Features: Prompts

This page has two parts. Part 1 is tied to the Nusantara Capital before deck and walks you through the workshop exercise. Part 2 is a set of prompts you can take away and use on any deck after the workshop.

Use these in **ChatGPT, Copilot Chat, or any AI chat tool** alongside PowerPoint.

---

## Part 1 — Workshop Exercise

Open the before deck before you start: [Download NC_Q3_2025_Before.pptx](../01-rethinking-presentations/NC_Q3_2025_Before.pptx)

### Step 1 — Rewrite all 12 slide titles

Open the before deck. Look at each slide title. Most are labels, not points. Run this prompt to get rewritten versions, then apply them directly in PowerPoint.

```
Here are the 12 slide titles from a corporate quarterly review deck.
Rewrite each one so it states a point rather than just labelling a topic.
Keep each title under 12 words. The audience is senior leadership at a
Malaysian investment holding company.

1. Q3 2025 Departmental Review (cover)
2. Agenda
3. Department Overview
4. Q3 Revenue Performance
5. Project Status Update
6. Key Achievements
7. Headcount and Staffing
8. Budget Utilisation
9. Risks and Issues
10. Q4 Priorities
11. Key Decisions Needed
12. Thank You
```

### Step 2 — Trim slide 3 (Department Overview)

Slide 3 has 8 bullet points, each 2 to 3 sentences long. This is one of the worst slides in the deck. Run this prompt to trim it.

```
Here is the content of slide 3 from a corporate quarterly review deck.
Title: "Department Overview"

Bullets:
- The Strategy and Planning department was established in 2018 and currently
  operates with a team of 14 full-time employees and 2 contract staff members
  who are engaged on a project basis.
- The department is responsible for corporate strategy, market intelligence,
  business development, performance monitoring, and stakeholder engagement
  across all business units.
- During Q3, the department managed a total of 7 active projects spanning
  4 different business units, with varying levels of complexity and resource
  requirements.
- Total department headcount has remained stable compared to Q2, with one
  resignation in August partially offset by an ongoing recruitment process
  for a replacement analyst.
- The department operates across two office locations in Kuala Lumpur and
  Cyberjaya, with remote working arrangements in place for 30% of staff on
  a rotational basis.
- Key stakeholder engagements in Q3 included quarterly reviews with 3
  business unit heads, 2 board-level briefings, and ongoing coordination
  with the Finance and Risk departments.
- The department's operating budget for FY2025 was approved at RM 3.2
  million, with Q3 spend tracking at RM 780K which represents 24% of the
  annual budget.
- Staff training and development activities in Q3 included 2 external
  training programmes attended by 6 staff members, with further training
  planned for Q4.

Rewrite this slide content. Rules:
1. Maximum 3 bullet points
2. Each bullet maximum 10 words
3. Remove anything the new title already covers
4. The new title is: "A stable department managing 7 active projects across 4 business units"
5. Audience: CEO and division heads
```

### Step 3 — Trim slide 11 (Key Decisions Needed)

This is the most important slide in the deck. The actual budget approval request is buried in bullet 4 of 6. Run this prompt to restructure it.

```
Here is slide 11 from a corporate quarterly review deck.
Title: "Key Decisions Needed"

Current bullets:
1. The department has reviewed its Q3 performance and identified several
   areas requiring leadership input and decision-making support to ensure
   smooth execution in Q4.
2. Guidance is requested on the appropriate communication approach for the
   Digital Roadmap findings, particularly regarding how results should be
   shared with external stakeholders.
3. Clarification is needed on the revised travel policy for Q4, specifically
   whether overseas client visits can resume following the earlier suspension.
4. Approval is required to reallocate one headcount from Project Angkasa to
   Project Rimau for a period of 8 weeks to address the current resource gap
   and prevent further delays.
5. The department also requests endorsement of the updated project governance
   framework which has been under review since June and requires sign-off to
   take effect.
6. Finally, the team would welcome guidance on budget flexibility for the Q4
   consultancy line, as two potential engagements may require spend above
   the current allocation.

Restructure this slide. Rules:
1. The headcount reallocation (currently bullet 4) must be the first and
   most prominent item. It is the only decision needed today.
2. Rewrite the title to make the ask explicit.
3. Maximum 3 items total.
4. Each item one sentence, maximum 12 words.
5. The audience is the CEO who has 2 minutes for this slide.
```

### Step 4 — Generate speaker notes for slide 5 (Project Status)

Slide 5 is the project status table. Run this prompt to generate useful speaker notes.

```
Write speaker notes for this PowerPoint slide.

Title: Two projects need a decision before Q4 begins
Content: A project status table showing 10 projects. Two are delayed
(Project Helang: 3 weeks behind due to vendor issues; Project Rimau:
resource gap since August resignation). One is at risk. Seven are on track.

Context: The presenter is a Strategy department head speaking to the CEO
and two division heads. This is a quarterly review. The next slide covers
budget utilisation. Time on this slide: about 2 minutes.

Format the notes as 3 short talking points, each one sentence, plus a
one-line transition to the next slide. Do not write a full script.
```

### Step 5 — Test PowerPoint Designer

After rewriting the title on slide 4 (Revenue Performance), open Designer in PowerPoint:

1. Click on slide 4
2. Go to Home tab, then Designer (right side panel)
3. Review the layout suggestions
4. Accept one that removes clutter, reject one that adds decorative icons

Then run this prompt to understand why some suggestions work and others do not:

```
I have a revenue slide with this content: one pie chart showing revenue
by 7 business units, a title "Revenue exceeded target for the second
quarter running", and one supporting line "Total Q3 revenue: RM 4.2M".
PowerPoint Designer is suggesting layouts with stock images and icon grids.

Which Designer suggestion should I accept and which should I reject?
What is the principle for deciding?
```

## Part 2 — Try It Yourself

Use these prompts on any deck you are working on outside the workshop.

### Rewriting slide titles

```
Here are the slide titles from my deck. Rewrite each one so it states a
point rather than just labelling a topic. Keep each title under 12 words.

[paste your slide titles]
```

```
My slide title is [paste your title]. The slide contains: [describe the
content]. Write 3 alternative titles that each emphasise a different angle.
```

### Trimming bullet point content

```
Here is the content of one of my slides. Rewrite the bullet points so
each one is a single sentence of no more than 10 words. Remove anything
that the title already covers. Do not add new information.

Title: [paste your title]
Bullets: [paste your bullets]
```

```
This slide has too many bullet points. Help me cut it down to the 3 most
important points for [describe your audience].

[paste your bullet points]
```

### Writing speaker notes

```
Write speaker notes for this PowerPoint slide.

Title: [paste your title]
Content: [describe or paste the slide content]

Context: The presenter is [describe role] speaking to [describe audience].
Tone: [describe tone]. Time on this slide: [X] minutes. The next slide
covers [describe next slide].

Format: 3 short talking points plus a one-line transition. Not a full script.
```

```
My speaker notes are too long and I end up reading from the screen. Here
are my notes: [paste notes]. Condense them into 4 short reminder phrases,
not full sentences.
```

### Working with PowerPoint Designer

```
I have a slide with this content: [describe your slide content].
PowerPoint Designer is suggesting [describe the suggestions]. Which layout
is most appropriate for this type of slide and why?
```

```
I want cleaner, more minimal layouts from PowerPoint Designer. My slides
are data-heavy and I do not want stock images or decorative icons. What
settings or approaches should I use?
```

## Copilot in PowerPoint — Slide Creation Prompt

Use this prompt directly in **Copilot in PowerPoint** to create a trainer slide deck for this module. Paste it into the Copilot chat panel inside PowerPoint.

```
Create a 10-slide trainer presentation for a workshop module titled
"Improving Slide Content with PowerPoint AI Features".

AUDIENCE: Corporate training participants with basic PowerPoint skills.
PURPOSE: Teach participants how to use AI to rewrite slide titles, trim
bullet content, use PowerPoint Designer, and generate speaker notes.
TONE: Professional, practical, direct. No filler. Every slide makes one point.

DESIGN DIRECTION:
- Colour palette: corporate navy (#1A3055), white backgrounds for content
  slides, dark navy for title and closing slides
- Typography: large bold serif titles (Georgia if available), clean sans-serif body
- Layout: vary layouts across slides. Use comparison tables, process flows,
  before/after columns, and single-stat callouts. No two consecutive slides
  should share the same layout.
- Every slide title must be an action statement, not a label
- Include a subheading on each content slide that elaborates on the title
- No decorative shapes, no stock images, no clipart

SLIDE CONTENT:

Slide 1 (Title, dark navy background):
Title: Module 03 — Improving Slide Content with PowerPoint AI Features
Subtitle: Fix the titles. Trim the bullets. Generate the notes.
Label: 50 minutes

Slide 2 (Opening question, dark background):
Title: What is the point of this slide?
Body: Show a slide title that says "Department Overview" with 8 dense bullet
points. Ask the audience what the point of the slide is. Use this as the
hook for the headline-first rule.

Slide 3 (Comparison table, white background):
Title: Slide titles should state a point, not label a topic
Subheading: The difference is whether the audience knows the conclusion
before reading the content
Show a two-column table: Weak (label) on the left, Strong (point) on the right
Rows: Q3 Revenue / Revenue exceeded target for the second quarter running |
Project Status / Two projects need a decision this week |
Team Overview / Headcount is stable but capacity is not |
Key Risks / Three risks require senior attention before Q4 begins

Slide 4 (Prompt example, white background):
Title: Being specific gets you 10x better AI output
Subheading: Vague prompts produce vague results — give AI the context it needs
Show two code blocks side by side: left block shows a vague prompt
("Improve this slide"), right block shows a specific prompt with title,
bullets, rules, and audience context

Slide 5 (Process flow, white background):
Title: Trim any slide in four steps
Subheading: Work through the Nusantara Capital before deck using this sequence
Show a horizontal 4-step flow: 1. Rewrite the title | 2. Cut bullets to 3
maximum, 10 words each | 3. Run Designer | 4. Generate speaker notes

Slide 6 (Before/after, white background):
Title: Designer fixes layout — it cannot fix a bad message
Subheading: Accept suggestions that reduce clutter. Reject suggestions
that add decoration.
Show two side-by-side slide thumbnails: before (busy layout with icons
and stock image) and after (clean layout with content only)

Slide 7 (Single stat callout, dark navy background):
Title: A slide with one big number needs one clean layout
Body: Large stat: RM 4.2M. Label: Q3 Revenue. Supporting line: 10% above
target. The point: do not let Designer decorate a number that speaks for itself.

Slide 8 (Speaker notes structure, white background):
Title: Good speaker notes have three parts — not a full script
Subheading: Most people either leave notes blank or write an essay.
Neither is useful.
Show a three-part structure with icons: 1. State the point of the slide
in one sentence | 2. List 2 to 3 talking points | 3. Add a transition to
the next slide

Slide 9 (Exercise slide, white background):
Title: Your turn — apply this to the before deck
Subheading: Open the Nusantara Capital deck and work through slides 3,
5, and 11 using the prompts on the workshop site
Show a numbered exercise checklist: 1. Rewrite all 12 titles |
2. Trim slide 3 bullets to 3 maximum | 3. Restructure slide 11 |
4. Generate speaker notes for slide 5

Slide 10 (Closing, dark navy background):
Title: Module 03 covered
List the 4 key takeaways:
1. Slide titles must state a point, not a label
2. AI trimming is a starting point — always edit the output
3. Designer fixes layout, not message
4. Speaker notes are talking points, not a script
Next module label: Module 04 — Visualising Data for Storytelling
```
