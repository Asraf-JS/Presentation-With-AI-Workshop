# 01 — Rethinking Presentations: Prompts

Use these in **ChatGPT, Copilot Chat, or any AI chat tool** to explore the concepts from this module.

---

## Diagnosing a weak deck

```
I have a corporate PowerPoint deck that I need to improve. It has 18 slides covering our department's quarterly performance. The slides are mostly bullet points, the charts are copied directly from Excel, and there is no clear takeaway. What are the 5 most common problems with corporate presentation decks like this, and what should I fix first?
```

```
Here is a slide from my deck. The title is "Q3 Department Performance Overview" and the content is: revenue RM 4.2M (target RM 3.8M), headcount 42, 3 new clients onboarded, 2 projects delayed, 1 staff resigned. What is wrong with this slide and how would you improve it?
```

---

## Understanding where AI helps

```
I am preparing a department presentation for senior leadership. Which parts of the presentation process should I use AI for, and which parts should I handle myself? Be specific about what AI is good at and where it falls short.
```

```
What are the limitations of using AI tools like PowerPoint Designer or Canva's AI features for corporate presentations? What should I be careful about?
```

---

## Framing the improvement project

```
I am about to improve an existing presentation deck rather than build one from scratch. The deck covers our department's quarterly results at a financial services organisation. Give me a simple checklist I should work through before I start making changes — covering message, structure, content, and design.
```

```
What is the difference between a presentation that informs and a presentation that persuades? Give me 3 concrete examples of how the same content would be structured differently depending on the goal.
```

```
I work in the Strategy department at Nusantara Capital, a Malaysian investment holding company. My department head has asked me to improve our Q3 quarterly review deck before it goes to the CEO and board. The deck currently has 20 slides of mostly bullet points and Excel charts. Where should I start?
```
