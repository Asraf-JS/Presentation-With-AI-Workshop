# 07 — AI-Assisted Review: Prompts

Use these to critique your deck before presenting. The more specific you are, the more useful the feedback.

---

## Reviewing narrative clarity

```
Here are the slide titles from my quarterly review presentation in order: [paste your titles]. Based on these titles alone, can you tell what the single key message of this presentation is? Where does the narrative logic break down or require a leap to follow?
```

```
I have a 12-slide presentation for senior leadership. The overall message is: "We had a strong Q3 and we need one decision to protect Q4 momentum." Here are my slide titles: [paste titles]. Does the slide sequence support this message? Which slides are unnecessary or misplaced?
```

---

## Finding overloaded slides

```
Here is a slide from my deck. Tell me if it is overloaded and what should be cut or split.

Title: Q3 Project and Team Update
Bullets:
- Project Helang is 3 weeks delayed due to vendor issues — escalation is in progress
- Project Rimau has a resource gap since the August resignation — we are interviewing replacements
- The team completed two training programmes in Q3 (Excel Advanced and Presentation Skills)
- One staff member received a performance award from the COO
- We are planning a team offsite in November for Q4 planning
- Headcount remains at 14 full-time, 2 contractors
```

```
I suspect some of my slides are redundant. Here is a summary of all my slides: [describe each slide in one sentence]. Identify which slides are making the same point or covering the same ground, and suggest which to cut.
```

---

## Improving slide headlines

```
Here are 8 slide titles from my deck. Identify which ones are too passive or vague and rewrite them to be more assertive and specific.

1. Overview of Q3
2. Strong revenue performance across all segments
3. Project Status
4. Some concerns in the operations team
5. Key decisions needed
6. Q4 outlook and priorities
7. Resource considerations
8. Summary
```

```
My slide title is "Resource considerations for Q4". The slide content argues that we need to move one headcount from Project A to Project B to avoid missing a deadline. Rewrite the title to be direct about what the slide is actually saying.
```

---

## Checking visual alignment

```
Here is a slide from my deck. The title says "Revenue exceeded target for the second quarter running." The visual is a stock photo of a city skyline at night. Is this visual aligned with the slide's message? What would be a more appropriate visual?
```

```
I have 3 chart slides in my deck. Here are the chart titles and what each chart shows: [describe each]. For each one, tell me whether the chart title makes a point or just labels the data, and rewrite any labels that need fixing.
```

---

## Final pass review

```
I am about to present my deck. Give me a 10-point final review checklist I should work through before I open the presentation in front of an audience. Focus on slide clarity, narrative flow, visual consistency, and delivery readiness.
```
