# 04 — Visualising Data: Prompts

Use these in **ChatGPT, Copilot Chat, or any AI chat tool** alongside your data slides.

---

## Choosing the right chart

```
I have the following data from my quarterly review and I need to decide which chart type to use for each one. Recommend a chart type for each and explain why in one sentence.

1. Revenue by quarter for the past 4 quarters (Q1–Q4)
2. Budget breakdown by department (6 departments)
3. Project completion percentage for 5 ongoing projects
4. Headcount changes over 12 months
5. Target vs actual for 3 KPIs
```

```
I am trying to show that one department is significantly outperforming the others in revenue. I have data for 6 departments. Should I use a pie chart, a bar chart, or something else? Explain the tradeoffs.
```

---

## Simplifying tables

```
I have a project status table with 12 rows (one per project) and these columns: Project Name, Owner, Start Date, Planned End Date, Actual End Date, Status (RAG), Budget Allocated, Budget Spent, Key Issues. I need to present this to the CEO in a 2-minute slide. Help me: (1) identify the most important information for this audience, (2) suggest which rows and columns to cut, and (3) write a title that makes a point.
```

```
Here is a data table from my deck: [paste your table]. The audience is senior leadership. What is the single most important insight in this data? How would you present it on one slide?
```

---

## Writing insight-driven chart titles

```
I have a bar chart showing monthly revenue for the past 6 months. The current title is "Monthly Revenue Jan–Jun". Rewrite the title to make a point. Here are the numbers: Jan 620K, Feb 580K, Mar 710K, Apr 690K, May 740K, Jun 820K. Give me 3 alternative titles with different angles.
```

```
My slide shows project completion rates across 5 departments. The current title is "Project Completion by Department Q3". Two departments are on track, two are slightly behind, and one is significantly behind. Write a title that communicates the key message without naming the underperforming department.
```

---

## Avoiding common chart mistakes

```
I have a 3D pie chart with 7 segments showing budget allocation. My trainer says I should replace it. What should I replace it with and how should I redesign the slide? The key point I want to make is that IT and Operations together make up 60% of the budget.
```

```
I have a dual-axis chart showing revenue (left axis) and headcount (right axis) over 8 quarters. My trainer says dual-axis charts are confusing. How should I split this into two clearer slides?
```
