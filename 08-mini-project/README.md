# 08 — Mini Project: End-to-End Slide Improvement

This is the practical capstone of the workshop. You take a real or provided deck and apply everything from Modules 01 through 07 to produce a finished, presentation-ready output.

> **Prompts to Try:** Open the [copy-paste prompt exercises](./prompts) for this module.

---

## What You Are Doing

You have two options:

**Option A — Improve the provided deck.**
A sample Nusantara Capital quarterly review deck is provided. It has the typical problems covered in this workshop: unclear titles, overloaded slides, raw Excel charts, inconsistent design. Your job is to improve it using the tools and techniques from every module.

**Option B — Improve your own deck.**
Bring a real deck from your organisation. It should have at least 10 slides and cover a topic where you understand the message and the audience.

---

## The Sequence

Work through this in order. Do not skip to design before the message is clear.

### Step 1 — Diagnose (Module 01 + 02)

Before changing anything, complete this brief:

```
AUDIENCE: [who is this for?]
PURPOSE: [inform / persuade / request a decision]
KEY MESSAGE: [one sentence]
BIGGEST PROBLEMS IN THE CURRENT DECK: [list 3]
```

If you cannot fill this in, stop and do Module 02 again before touching a slide.

### Step 2 — Restructure (Module 02)

Plan the storyline. Decide which slides to keep, which to cut, and what order they should be in. You do not have to do this in PowerPoint — a list on paper or in a notes app is faster.

### Step 3 — Rewrite (Module 03)

Fix every slide title. Trim bullet points. Generate speaker notes for the 3-5 most important slides.

### Step 4 — Fix the Data Slides (Module 04)

For every chart or table slide: write an insight-driven title, remove unnecessary data, and check that the chart type matches the message.

### Step 5 — Redesign Key Slides in Canva (Module 05)

Choose 2-3 slides for a Canva treatment. Build them, export as PNG, and insert into the deck.

### Step 6 — Consistency Pass (Module 06)

Go through the full deck. Check fonts, colours, spacing. Make sure the Canva slides and PowerPoint slides look like they belong together.

### Step 7 — AI Review (Module 07)

Run your slide titles through an AI critique prompt. Address anything that comes back unclear or misaligned.

---

## Peer Review

When you are ready, you will present the improved deck to a small group (2-3 people). The review focuses on three things:

**Message clarity:** Can the reviewer state the key message in one sentence after watching the deck?

**Slide quality:** Are there any slides that are still overloaded, unclear, or inconsistent?

**Visual consistency:** Does the deck look like one piece of work?

Use the peer review form below. Reviewers fill it in while watching the presentation — not after.

---

## Peer Review Form

| Question | Reviewer response |
|----------|-------------------|
| What is the key message of this presentation? (one sentence) | |

## After the Workshop

The techniques and prompts on this site do not expire when the workshop ends. The most useful ones to keep using:

- The audience-purpose-message brief before starting any new deck
- The insight-driven title rule for every slide
- The AI narrative critique prompt (paste your slide titles and ask if the story is clear)
- The final review checklist from Module 07

If you work on presentations regularly, building these as habits — rather than a one-time workshop exercise — is where the real improvement happens.
