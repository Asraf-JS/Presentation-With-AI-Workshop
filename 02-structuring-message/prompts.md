# 02 — Structuring Your Message: Prompts

This page has two parts. Part 1 is tied to the Nusantara Capital before deck and walks you through the workshop exercise. Part 2 is a set of prompts you can take away and use on any deck after the workshop.

Use these in **ChatGPT, Copilot Chat, or any AI chat tool**.

---

## Part 1 — Workshop Exercise

Open the before deck before you start: [Download NC_Q3_2025_Before.pptx](../01-rethinking-presentations/NC_Q3_2025_Before.pptx)

### Step 1 — Identify the missing message

Look at slide 2 (Agenda) and slide 11 (Key Decisions Needed) of the before deck, then run this prompt.

```
Here is the agenda from a 12-slide corporate quarterly review deck for a Malaysian investment holding company:

1. Department Overview
2. Q3 Revenue Performance
3. Project Status Update
4. Key Achievements
5. Headcount and Staffing
6. Budget Utilisation
7. Risks and Issues
8. Q4 Priorities
9. Key Decisions Required

Based on this agenda alone, what is the key message of this presentation? What decision or action is the audience being asked to take? If you cannot tell, explain why the agenda structure is the problem.
```

### Step 2 — Find the buried ask

Look at slide 11 (Key Decisions Needed) in the before deck. Read through all 6 bullet points, then run this prompt.

```
Here are the 6 bullet points from a slide titled "Key Decisions Needed" in a corporate quarterly review:

1. The department has reviewed its Q3 performance and identified several areas requiring leadership input and decision-making support to ensure smooth execution in Q4.
2. Guidance is requested on the appropriate communication approach for the Digital Roadmap findings, particularly regarding how results should be shared with external stakeholders.
3. Clarification is needed on the revised travel policy for Q4, specifically whether overseas client visits can resume following the earlier suspension.
4. Approval is required to reallocate one headcount from Project Angkasa to Project Rimau for a period of 8 weeks to address the current resource gap and prevent further delays.
5. The department also requests endorsement of the updated project governance framework which has been under review since June and requires sign-off to take effect.
6. Finally, the team would welcome guidance on budget flexibility for the Q4 consultancy line, as two potential engagements may require spend above the current allocation.

Which of these is the most critical decision? Why is it a problem that it appears in bullet point 4? How should this slide be restructured?
```

### Step 3 — Apply the SCR structure to the before deck

Now that you have identified the message and the buried ask, run this prompt.

```
I have a 12-slide quarterly review deck for a Malaysian investment holding company. The current slide order is: Cover, Agenda, Department Overview, Q3 Revenue, Project Status, Key Achievements, Headcount, Budget, Risks, Q4 Priorities, Key Decisions, Thank You.

The most important message is: we exceeded Q3 revenue targets and need approval to reallocate one headcount from Project Angkasa to Project Rimau to prevent Q4 delays.

Using the Situation-Complication-Resolution structure, suggest a revised slide order that puts the message first and earns the data slides. Keep the same 12 slides but reorder and rename them.
```

### Step 4 — Write the key message sentence

```
Here are the Q3 results for the Nusantara Capital Strategy department:

- Revenue: RM 4.2M actual vs RM 3.8M target (10% above plan)
- Projects: 7 active, 2 delayed, 1 at risk
- Headcount: 1 resignation in August, replacement being recruited
- Key ask: reallocate 1 headcount from Project Angkasa to Project Rimau for 8 weeks

Write a single-sentence key message for a 15-minute presentation to the CEO and two division heads. The purpose is to get approval for the reallocation. Give me 3 versions with different angles.
```

### Step 5 — Fill in the planning template

```
I am about to restructure the Nusantara Capital Q3 quarterly review deck. Help me fill in this planning template:

AUDIENCE: CEO and two division heads at Nusantara Capital, a Malaysian investment holding company
PURPOSE: Report Q3 results and get approval for one headcount reallocation
KEY MESSAGE: [suggest the best version based on the results above]

Give me a suggested storyline using the SCR structure with 8 to 10 slide beats and a draft headline for each beat.
```

## Part 2 — Try It Yourself

Use these prompts on any deck you are working on outside the workshop.

### Defining audience and purpose

```
I am preparing a presentation for [describe your audience and their role]. They need to [describe the decision or outcome you need]. Help me identify: (1) what they care most about, (2) what objections they are likely to raise, and (3) what a successful outcome from this presentation looks like.
```

```
I have a presentation where the purpose is to report progress, but I also need approval for something. How should I structure the deck differently depending on whether the primary goal is reporting or requesting?
```

### Writing a key message

```
Here are my key results: [paste your data or summary]. Write a single-sentence key message for a presentation to senior leadership that captures what matters most. Give me 3 versions with different angles.
```

```
My presentation covers too many topics and I am struggling to find a single key message. Here are my main points: [paste your points]. Help me identify which one should be the spine of the presentation and how the others should relate to it.
```

### Building a storyline

```
I need to present [describe your topic] to [describe your audience] and request [describe what you need]. Use the Situation-Complication-Resolution structure to help me plan the narrative arc. Give me a suggested flow with 6 to 8 slide beats and a headline for each beat.
```

```
Here is my current slide order: [paste your slide titles]. Evaluate whether this follows a clear narrative arc. Identify where the story breaks down and suggest a reordered version.
```

### Data storytelling

```
I have a slide with this data: [paste your numbers]. The slide title is currently [paste your title]. Write me 3 alternative titles that tell a story instead of just labelling the data.
```

```
I have a table with [describe your table]. I need to turn this into a single clear insight for a leadership presentation. Help me identify the most important point in this data and suggest how to present it on one slide.
```

### Planning before opening PowerPoint

```
I am about to build a [X]-minute presentation for [describe the occasion and audience]. Before I open PowerPoint, help me fill in this planning template:

AUDIENCE: [describe who will be in the room]
PURPOSE: [inform / persuade / request a decision / report progress]
KEY MESSAGE: [describe your results or situation and ask AI to suggest the message]

Give me a suggested storyline with [X] beats and a draft headline for each.
```
